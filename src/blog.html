Our rules and policies

last updated 5/5/2023

Our rules can be summed up in our motto: "Play with respect, compete with fairness."

Our rules and policies are an essential component that outlines the expectations and regulations that govern the behavior of club members. It is important for the chess club to have clear and concise rules to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for all participants. The following paragraphs will detail some typical rules and policies found on the webpage of a chess club for young people.

  1. Respectful behaviour

    One of the most important rules outlining the rules and policies of the chess club is the requirement for respectful behaviour. Members are expected to treat each other with kindness and respect and to refrain from any form of bullying or harassment. This policy helps to create a welcoming and inclusive environment where all members can feel comfortable and safe.

  2. Being there is essential

    All members are expected to follow the guidelines set out by the club's organizers. This includes adhering to the schedule of meetings, participating in events and activities, and following the instructions of the coaches and mentors. Members who repeatedly fail to follow the guidelines may face disciplinary action, including suspension or expulsion from the club.

  3. Conflicts are resolved in civilised terms

    Members should be encouraged to communicate any issues or concerns they have with each other directly and respectfully. If the issue cannot be resolved through direct communication, members can approach a coach or mentor who can act as a mediator. If the issue still cannot be resolved, the matter can be escalated to a higher authority, such as the club president or board of directors. In cases of serious misconduct or violation of the club's rules and policies, disciplinary action may be taken, including suspension or expulsion from the club

Not allowing conflics to escalate is the responsability of every member of the club. We should be able to maintain a level-headed attitude regardless of the competitive nature of the sport.

The conflict resolution policy ensures that any issues or concerns can be resolved fairly and efficiently, while maintaining a positive and inclusive atmosphere for all participants. The chess club is committed to providing a positive and educational experience for young people interested in the game of chess, and encourages all members to approach their participation with enthusiasm, good sportsmanship, and a commitment to excellence.

It is important to note that the rules and policies of the chess club are not meant to be punitive or intimidating, but rather are intended to ensure a positive and respectful environment for all members. The club organizers take these rules seriously and are committed to enforcing them consistently and fairly. However, the club recognizes that mistakes can happen, and is willing to work with members to address any issues that may arise. Ultimately, the goal of the club is to provide a supportive and welcoming community for young people to learn, grow, and enjoy the game of chess.

In summary, the rules and policies of the chess club provide a framework for members to engage in fair and respectful competition while upholding the values of good sportsmanship and respect for others. These guidelines help to ensure that all members have an enjoyable and fulfilling experience while participating in the club's activities. By adhering to these rules, members can develop important skills, such as critical thinking and problem-solving, while fostering a sense of community and camaraderie.
